Chameleon

Interesting facts about chameleon!

  • Chameleons are reptiles that are part of the iguans suborder

  • Most chameleons have a prehensile tail that they use to wrap around tree branches

Camel

Interesting facts about Camel!

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels can move easily across the sand because of its specially designed feet
  • Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!

Cicada

Interesting facts about Cicada!

  • The cicadas are a superfamily, the Cicadoidea
  • There are more than 3000 species of cicadas all over the world
  • They have an exceptionally loud song, produced in most species by the rapid buckling and unbuckling of drumlike tymbals

Teddy

Interesting facts about Teddy!

  • Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  • The first toy stuffed bear was created by German toymaker Margarete Steiff. She created a soft plush with movable arms and legs and a friendly face after taking her nephew to the zoo in 1903.
  • The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall! It was created by South African artist Cheryl Moss who is known for her minuscule work.
